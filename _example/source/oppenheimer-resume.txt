Plot
  The movie is a biographical drama about the life of J. Robert Oppenheimer, the American physicist who played a key role in the development of the atomic bomb. The film covers Oppenheimer's time in university, his leadership of the Los Alamos Laboratory during the Manhattan Project, and his 1954 security hearing.

Director
  Christopher Nolan wrote, produced, and directed the movie.

Cast
  Cillian Murphy stars as Oppenheimer, and Robert Downey Jr. plays Lewis Strauss, a member of the United States Atomic Energy Commission.

Based on
  The movie is based on the 2005 Pulitzer Prize-winning biography American Prometheus by Kai Bird and Martin J. Sherwin.

Critical reception
  The movie was well-received by critics, who praised its complex portrayal of Oppenheimer and Nolan's technical accomplishments.

Box office
  The movie was a commercial success, grossing over $900 million in the first four months after its release.

Production
  The movie was produced by Universal Pictures, Atlas Entertainment, and Gadget Films.
